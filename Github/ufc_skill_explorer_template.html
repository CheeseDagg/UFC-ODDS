What each rating means & how to read it
Strikingsignificant strikes landed per minute (opponent-adjusted) blended with strike accuracy — both volume and precision on the feet. Power is rated separately.
Striking defenseshare of opponents' significant strikes that miss — how hard a fighter is to hit cleanly.
Wrestlingtakedowns landed plus takedown defense, opponent-adjusted.
Grapplingcontrol time plus submission threat, opponent-adjusted.
PowerKO/TKO finishes and knockdowns, discounted by how durable the opponents were, then scaled by weight class: heavier divisions hit harder in absolute terms, so a 50 here means more for a heavyweight than a flyweight. This is the one axis built to compare across divisions.
Chinresistance to being finished by strikes, weighted by how dangerous the opponents were.
Cardiosustained work rate in championship rounds (3+), counting striking volume and grappling control — so a wrestler who grinds out late rounds gets credit, not just a high-output striker. Fighters with only 1-2 championship rounds get a provisional rating (pulled toward average); fighters who've never reached round 3 aren't rated on cardio at all. Both are flagged on the card.
Every axis except Power is normalized within each weight class (50 = division average), opponent-adjusted, recency-weighted, and built on recorded fight data only — so it describes a fighter relative to their own division. Power alone is weight-adjusted to be comparable across divisions.

Model report card

How the model’s picks actually do against the market. Every fight on the card is tracked, grouped by event. Rated same-division bouts get the model’s win‑probability pick; prospect and cross-division bouts instead show a grade lean (which fighter the 7 axes rate higher — no probability) scored in its own separate tally. For prospect bouts the lean also factors in age — a thin UFC record can’t tell a rising 24-year-old from a declining 35-year-old import, so the younger fighter gets a nudge; this was walk-forward cross-validated to add ~5% accuracy out-of-sample and is display-only (it never touches the win model). Genuine debuts have no read. Mark each winner as the card settles — the tally is saved in this browser and carries across cards, building real evidence over time. Use the × on any card to remove it.
